Designing the Soapomorph

#group work #cad #3d modelling #adobe illustrator #inkscape
Posted on 19 April 2026

Stakeholders

A stakeholder is an individual, group or organisation that has an interest in a product or service, can influence its decisions, or is affected by its outcomes and/or deliverables (Stakeholder | Glossary, no date).

The stakeholders requiring consideration when developing the Soapomorph are detailed in the table below.

Stakeholder Interest or Influence Action
Bathroom cleaner Needs to clean and refill it safely and efficiently Accessible hatch for refilling and easily cleanable surfaces
Guests Needs to be able to safely and easily interact with the mechanism Zero-contact motion sensor, non-toxic surfaces, properly secured in place
Venue owners Needs return on investment, safety for guests, easy disposal Price of manufacture is fair relative to cost, disposable, sustainable materials, as above
Everyone Needs environment to not be polluted Sustainable materials, convenient to dispose of properly

Specifications

The device is a soap dispenser with a loosely-Xenomorph-inspired design. It should have no toxic or sharp features on the surfaces. It should be easy to clean and dispose of at its end of life. The skull component that touch the soap should be at least somewhat resistant to water and weak alkalise such as soap.

Design of Components

Skull, Jaw, Tongue, and Mounting Plate

The front of the skull with an exposed brain was 3D scanned from a ceramic ornament. This was advantageous because the detailed work was already done, streamlining the process of designing the most visible part of the device.

Blender was used to model the back into a more Xenomorph-like shape. Blender was suitable for this because of the artistic-nature of this outermost component. This enlarged it to the point of unfeasibility for printing on the 3D factory’s printers. It was split into four parts, with pins for easy assembly and adhesion.

The jaw and tongue were likewise modelled in Blender, with hinging joints to allow the mechanism to move freely.

Mechanism

A mechanical actuator was designed to push the tongue forwards and open the jaw simultaneously. Space was left behind to fit the servo motor to drive it forwards.

Base/Stand

Some parts were cut from MDF by hand for sturdiness, while the back was designed in Illustrator to be laser cut from 3mm plywood.
