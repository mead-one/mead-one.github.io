Building the Soapomorph

#group work #3d printing #woodworking
Posted on 13 May 2026

While the CAD and STL files were completed, lack of availability of resources, such as the 3D Factory’s 3D printers and laser cutters, meant that not all components could be manufactured within the project’s timeframe.

Skull, Jaw and Tongue

As a substitute for the topmost, 3D printed components, an animation was produced in Blender to simulate the movement of the jaw and tongue, shown in Figure 1.

Due to time and experience constraints, the jaw and tongue do not move simultaneously like the actual mechanism inherently would, but it provides a useful demonstration of the mechanism’s movement and the detachable lid. It also provides a preview of the visibility of the join, where the separately printed back and front of the skull meet.

Base

The lack of availability of laser cutting facilities was accounted for by assembling a base from parts cut from MDF and plywood, as shown in Figure 2.

The door opens easily and smoothly, albeit without an ergonomically designed handle to ease the user in gripping it, and the compartment comfortably fits a typically sized bottle of hand soap.
